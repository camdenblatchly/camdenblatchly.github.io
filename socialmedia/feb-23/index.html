How to make an outdoor media youtube film

Your film should be 5-14 minutes long.

It should share the story of a highly-trained athlete completing an arduous mountain challenge. The highly-trained athlete should be a man and, ideally, he should struggle with the risk of his outdoor pursuits and the impact his death could have on his wife and four year-old son, ideally named Cedar, Forrest, or Wren. His wife or girlfriend should confess that she wishes he would stop doing these risky mountain pursuits, but then acknowledge that he just wouldn’t be himself otherwise.

Your film should be serious. When asked why he does what he does, the athlete should say something generic about the beauty in not knowing if a challenge is possible. Throughout the film, the athlete should constantly express his excitement about the activity in a monotone voice. The soundtrack should be tense and dramatic. The athlete should never smile or make a joke about the absurdity of the contrived challenge he is pursuing.

Or your film should be irreverent and fun! The athletes should all wear tutus and retro 80s clothing. The men should have mustaches that they refer to as their “adventurestache.” They should do a big outdoor adventure wearing jorts and then celebrate with a beer in the parking lot afterwards. They should refer to their big outdoor adventure as a sufferfest.

Or your film should have a purpose. The athlete should do a mountain challenge to bring awareness to an issue. Awareness will solve the issue. The athlete should have an amazing experience doing the challenge, and, afterwards, reflect on the power of community and the importance of protecting wild places.

Or your film should highlight a role model. It should share the story of a simple person who has dedicated their life to conservation. The film should be filled with beautiful shots of this hero procuring plant samples from high on a cliffside, even though the vast majority of the hero’s days are spent working at a computer in an office. The film should make us feel bad about the meaninglessness of our jobs and feel great about the brand that sponsored the video.

Congrats, you've now made an outdoor media youtube film. Don't forget to break it into clips for TikTok, promote it aggressively on Instagram, and premier it at your main sponsor's retail location in New York.
